To appreciate each other's existence

In the real world, if you happen to see me, you will find a somewhat aloof young man with a slightly hunched back (common computer users L). His mind just went through a radical change, and he now appreciate the company of others much more than he used to...

My name is Bach. In Vietnamese, it means 100 (the number, yes), and in German (why also yes) it depicts a small stream of water. But I usually ask others to call me Buck, which in my mental representation usually includes one dollar and the operator Buck from the shooting video game Rainbow Six Siege.

So, what did I learn from my two years of studying abroad from the MA program in Hungary? Quite a lot, for my own personal growth. Not much, for the "career-oriented" future that society demands.

Posing for the camera!


But I will list a couple of things that I deem to be the most important and life-chaning during my time there

  1. The world is far more complex than I originally perceive it to be. Progress in economics and technological evolution does not mean all misery on earth will magically disappear one day.
  2. Meaningful relationships are far more important than fleeing attachment to short-lived objects and video games that I could buy online.
  3. Loneliness eats you alive from inside out. I made the mistake of thinking one man can find solace and pleasure in the digital fantasy, but social interactions in real life are much more valuable to my mental wellbeing.
  4. The friends I made from afar... I long to see them again already. Some came from war-torn regions, some came from third-world countries. But everyone was there, at that point in time, to seek a better future, for themselves, and the society.
  5. The shared truth and reality can unite people across boundaries and cultures.
